
TEMPLES OF ONDIPUDUR, COIMBATORE - SEARCH DATASET

Ondipudur is a major suburb in Coimbatore known for its cultural temples and community festivals.

ARULMIGU MARIAMMAN KOVIL - ONDIPUDUR
Mariamman is the goddess of rain and disease protection.
This temple is considered the Gramadeivatha (Village Deity).
Festival: Annual Thiruvila during Panguni / Chithirai.
Rituals:
- Fire Walking (Pookuzhi)
- Kambam Naduthal using neem leaves and sacred water

ARULMIGU SENGODAMMAN KOVIL - ONDIPUDUR
Sengodamman is a powerful folk ancestral deity.
People visit for family blessings and protection.
Festivals:
- Aadi Velli Special Pooja
- Annadhanam Community Food Offering

ARULMIGU VEERAMASTHI AMMAN KOVIL - ONDIPUDUR
Associated with bravery, sacrifice and protection.
Connected historically to Maha Sati tradition.
Used for obstacle removal prayers and family wellness poojas.

ARULMIGU SOWDESWARI AMMAN KOVIL - ONDIPUDUR
Main deity of Devanga weaving community.
Known for Sword Ritual (Kathi Haakuva).
Festival: Maha Parameswari Festival.
Cultural importance for weaving community lineage.

GENERAL ONDIPUDUR CULTURE NOTES
Ondipudur is culturally connected with Coimbatore traditional Tamil festivals.
Most temples conduct:
- Abhishekam
- Annadhanam
- Amman Festivals
- Community Processions
